THE EVOLUTION OF EQUALITY OF GENDER

During the eighteenth century and especially during the French Revolution, the goal of women was to live legally to live independent and free. They have been able to embody values ​​of freedom, equality and fraternity. Women revolt, petition, claim their political, civil and professional equality. They ask for girls and young people women a similar education to that given to boys, an equal access to higher education and equal opportunities through training. They require solutions to reduce the mortality in layers . FEMINISM TODAY

What has happened since the early 1990s, when feminism was exhausted and out of season, is the emergence of new forms of feminism. They force us to rethink feminisms as much as the social movements and to draw the weaknesses but also the new horizons in the national and international space.
